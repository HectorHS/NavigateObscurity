Last updated 29/03/2020.

Below you can see a snapshot of the current situation. Due to some data gathering lag, the data is about 1-2 days older than the last update date. The 4 main metrics are the confirmed coronavirus cases (Confirmed), deaths following a coronavirus infection (Deaths), complete recoveries following a coronavirus infection (Recovered), and currently infected people (Active), i.e. confirmed cases that have not been fatal, nor have they recovered yet. Apart from absolute numbers, I am also providing these metrics per 10,000 people to get a better idea of how impacted a country is. 1000 deaths in a country with a 5m population means something very different to the same number of deaths in a country with a 500m population. Lastly, I am providing two rates, death rate: the ratio of cases that develop to fatalities, and recovery rate: the ratio of recovered patients to the total cases.

Note that these numbers give us a decent overview of the situation, but are still far from accurate. The number of confirmed cases depends heavily on the extend of testing each country deploys and that varies widely. In addition, there is mounting evidence that a large proportion of infected people never develop any symptoms (around 50% in Iceland, 50% in an Italian example, 30% at a Japanese case study, 18% in Princess Diamond), which means it’s less likely to even think to get tested. All this tells us that the actual cases are much more numerous than the confirmed cases (a study argues that about 86% of cases where undetected in pre lockdown Wuhan). The number of deaths, however, should be much more accurate.
